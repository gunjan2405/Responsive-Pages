JAWAHAR LAL NEHRU

An Indian independence activist and, subsequently, the first Prime Minister of India, as well as a central figure in Indian politics both before and after independence.

Official portrait, 1947

Here's a time line of Jawaharlal Nehru:

  • 14 November 1889:Born in Allahabad to Motilal Nehru and Swaruprani
  • 1905-05:Studied at the Harrow School, Middlesex.
  • Oct-07:Went to Trinity College, Cambridge and studied Natural Science
  • Aug-12:Returned to India. Enrolled as an advocate of the Allahabad High Court
  • First World-War: Joined St John Ambulance and worked as one of its provincial secretaries in Allahabad
  • 1919:Joined Gandhiji in protest against Jallianwala Massacre
  • 1921:Imprisoned for the first time by the British
  • 1923:elected chairman of the Allahabad Municipal Board in 1923
  • 1938:Became Chairman of the National Planning Committee
  • 1946: Interim government formed 2 September with Nehru as VicePresident of the Viceroy's Executive Council and member for External Affairs and Commonwealth Relations
  • May-1947:declared that any princely state which refused to join the Constituent Assembly would be treated as an enemy state
  • Aug-1947: Became the first Prime Minister of Independent India
  • 1950: Became the Chairman of the Planning Commission
  • 1960 : Addressed the United Nations General Assembly in New York.
  • May-1964: Died in New Delhi

"Nehru served as prime minister for 18 years, first as the interim prime minister and from 1950 as the prime minister of the republic of India."
